# Information science

This branch derives information science from generated Fold support and exact
observation structure. It does not assume a bit, stochastic source,
probability distribution, logarithmic entropy, channel metric, code distance,
complex amplitude or quantum operation.

The dependency-ordered inventory is:

1. symbols and distinguishability;
2. encoding and decoding;
3. information quantity;
4. entropy and uncertainty;
5. compression and redundancy;
6. channels and capacity;
7. noise and error;
8. coding theory;
9. mutual and conditional information;
10. information conservation, loss and transformation;
11. classical and probabilistic information correspondence; and
12. quantum-information support correspondence.

Every law declares its complete finite grammar, exhaustive candidate product,
sole all-preserving survivor, operational witnesses, depth-independent
successor certificate, adverse controls and exact limitations. Quantum scope
here stops at information support: amplitude, phase, interference,
entanglement, gates, circuit dynamics, error correction and fault tolerance
remain obligations of the later quantum-computation branch.

The canonical catalog is `sft.information_science.catalog.SPECS`. Claim packages
and independent validators are materialized under `claims/` only through the
single admission engine.
