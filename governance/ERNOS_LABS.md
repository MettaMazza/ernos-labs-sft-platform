# Ernos Labs open-science governance

Ernos Labs is an open scientific movement under Maria Smith's scientific and
publication authority. It is intended to let independent researchers derive,
replicate, challenge and extend SFT through one shared evidentiary constitution.

## Permission to use the designation

Researchers may use the Ernos Labs designation for a project only if it:

- publishes the applicable SFT constitution;
- names every imported dependency and provenance class;
- does not import target answers or fitted parameters into law selection;
- generates and preserves candidate spaces and rejected alternatives;
- publishes source, controls, receipts and reproducible evidence;
- distinguishes proof, computation, empirical measurement and frontier;
- preserves unfavorable results; and
- accepts good-faith technical review.

The designation is withdrawn from work that knowingly bypasses these
conditions or uses the name to imply approval that was not granted.

## Gate roles

- **Submitter:** registers and supplies the complete evidence package.
- **Mechanical reviewers:** reproduce schemas, execution, controls and hashes.
- **Subject reviewers:** inspect scientific meaning and empirical sufficiency.
- **Gate maintainers:** open or close repository admission based on documented
  evidence.
- **Maria Smith:** retains scientific authorship, official-run selection and
  publication authority.

Credentials help route subject review. They do not decide mathematical truth or
override a reproducible failed control.

## Review conduct

Criticism must identify the exact statement, dependency, generator, form,
candidate, artifact, protocol, metric or boundary in dispute. Personal status
does not substitute for technical evidence.
