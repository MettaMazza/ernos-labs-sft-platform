## Abstract

This paper reports the completed foundation branch of the third clean-room
reconstruction of Smithian Fold Theory (SFT). The reconstruction begins with one
premise-free operational theorem - *there is no nothing* - and derives, in
dependency order, the structural One, exact positive finite count, exact
positive part coordinates, cross-partition part equivalence, the minimal Fold,
complete finite Fold-word support, the recursive foundational form grammar,
canonical form identity and the one-way boundary from exact derivation to
conventional measurement. No conventional number axioms, numerical zero,
negative quantity, irrational or imaginary proof value, floating-point proof,
completed infinity, fitted parameter, trained weight, target answer or earlier
SFT result is admitted as a premise.

Each result is processed by one fail-closed admission engine. Admission requires
source-bound registration, a generated complete candidate grammar, one decision
per candidate, exactly one survivor, minimality, named-shape uniqueness, an
exact boundary, four adverse-control classes, cryptographic sealing and
implementation-distinct recomputation. The ten claims execute 2,450 generated
candidate classes. Each claim has depth-independent closure and an independently
replicated engine receipt. The complete repository verification executes 96
unit, adverse-control and end-to-end tests, observes all 1,264 executable lines
of the 15-module core engine, and freshly recomputes all ten claims in order.

The result is deliberately foundational. It does not claim the later branches
of mathematics, information, computation, physics, chemistry or biology. It
establishes the exact objects and evidence boundary those branches must cite.
Every candidate, rejection, proof identity, control, certificate and receipt is
published as machine-readable evidence alongside this paper.

## 3. What “forced” means here

Forcing is not satisfied by naming plausible ingredients. A result is admitted
only after the complete declared same-grammar candidate domain has been
generated and executed. A candidate generator states its dimensions and exact
boundary. Every generated candidate has a stable identifier and trace hash.
Every candidate receives a survival decision, reason and proof hash. Exactly one
candidate must survive.

The survivor must also pass two distinct structural tests. Minimality rejects a
form when removing an element preserves the required result or when the form
contains an unforced addition. Named-shape uniqueness rejects a form when a
different same-boundary structure also satisfies the requirements. A finite
enumeration alone is conditional; every claim in this paper also carries a
base/successor or structural-induction certificate establishing that its
classification does not depend on a maximum execution depth.

Four controls are mandatory: a false premise, changed source, tampered artifact
and boundary violation. A separate process with an implementation hash distinct
from the derivation source must regenerate the candidate identities and unique
result from declared inputs. The engine seals the registration, census,
decisions, closure and controls before accepting that external certificate.

## 10. Derivation VI: cross-partition part equivalence

### 10.1 Why conventional reduction is not imported

Held/whole coordinates from different equal partitions may identify the same
part. Conventional cross-multiplication, division, decimal comparison and
fraction reduction are not premises. Equivalence is reconstructed from generated

### 10.2 Pair-cell common refinement

For every generated fibre label of partition A, the generator emits one cell
with every generated fibre label of partition B. Each cell retains the ordered
pair of source labels. Grouping cells by their first label refines A; grouping
by their second label refines B. This is nested finite generation, not an
imported multiplication law.

The held selection of A lifts to every pair cell descending from a held A fibre.
The selection of B is lifted symmetrically. The coordinates identify the same
part precisely when their lifted selected traces admit a complete one-to-one and
onto pairing.

### 10.3 The 1,024-form witness grammar

The generator classifies refinement coverage, overlap, fibre equality,
refinement of A, refinement of B, complete lifting of both selections, four
pairing classes and extra-data presence. Exactly one witness form survives:
complete, disjoint, equal, doubly refining, doubly lifted, bijective and
addition-free.

### 10.4 Equivalence laws

An identity pairing establishes reflexivity. Reversing every pair establishes
symmetry. Chaining the unique mates of two bijections establishes transitivity.
Uniform further refinement appends the same complete descendant construction on
both sides and preserves pairing existence. A generated one-of-two versus
two-of-four example pairs completely; a one-of-two versus one-of-three control
does not.

## 18. Limitations and next boundary

Foundation closure does not mean that all mathematics or science has been
derived. No arithmetic operation, algebra, probability law, automaton,
complexity class, physical constant, quantum gate or biological relation is
claimed in this paper. The part structure provides exact coordinates and
equivalence witnesses, not yet the entire arithmetic of those coordinates.

All generality claims concern generated finite objects. The theory does not
install a completed infinity or ungenerated continuum. Depth-independent means
that a base/successor or structural-induction certificate covers every generated
finite depth; it does not mean that an infinite object has been completed.

The next branch is mathematical foundations, beginning with exact arithmetic
